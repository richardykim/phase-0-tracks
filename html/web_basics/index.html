Seals

All About Seals

Description
Seals are carnivorous semi-aquatic marine mammals that live primarily in the polar and subpolar regions of the world. They spend the majority of their lives in water feeding mostly on fish and cephalopods. Though very cute and harmless looking at first glance, they are strong predators that eat their prey whole. Prey that are too large to eat at once are brought to the surface to be torn apart. Seals themselves are subject to predation, usually targeted by the killer whale or orca.

Types of Seals

  • Elephant seal
  • Harp seal
  • Harbor seal
  • Grey seal
  • Spotted seal
  • Monk seal
  • Leapord seal

These are just several of the many types of seals in the world

Life of a Seal
Typical life cycles of seals consist of:

  1. A female seal becoming pregnant. This normally happens when the seal is 3-5 years old.
  2. The female seal gives birth to a pup. Because they are relatively helpless, pups rely on their mothers for food and protection.
  3. Pups grow very quickly, depending on the types of seal.
  4. Most seals can live as long as 30 years old. Some species are known to live as long as even 45 years.
